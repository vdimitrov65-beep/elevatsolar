Green Energy in SEE — Baseline

Green Energy Brief is a monthly newsletter by Elevat Solar Consulting, dedicated to renewable energy markets in Southeast Europe. We track installed capacity, grid access, BESS development and policy shifts across 8 countries in the region.

Written for developers, investors and EPC companies operating or planning to operate in SEE. Free of charge, no paywalls.

Why a baseline?

Before tracking change, you need to know where things stand. This first Brief establishes the baseline — installed PV, wind and hydro capacity across the region, and the current state of battery storage deployment in each country.

The picture is uneven. Greece has crossed 12 GW of PV. Romania and Bulgaria are not far behind. The Western Balkans are just getting started. BESS is active in some markets and completely absent in others. This is where Southeast Europe stands in March 2026.

Why we publish this

We produce Green Energy Brief because we believe that accelerating the clean energy transition in Southeast Europe matters — for the industry, for the region, and for the kind of environment we all want to live in. Transparent, accessible data is a small but real contribution to that goal.

We apologise in advance for any inaccuracies in the data presented. We do our best to source reliable figures, but energy statistics across SEE markets are not always consistent. If you spot an error, please write to us at [email protected] — we will correct it in the next issue.
